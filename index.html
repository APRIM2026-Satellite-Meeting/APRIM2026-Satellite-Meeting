APRIM2026 Satellite Meeting

Precision Engineering for Space Exploration: Robotics, Autonomy and Instrumentation for Lunar, Planetary and Small Body Missions

Organized by the State Key Laboratory of Ultra-precision Machining Technology, Research Centre for Deep Space Explorations (RCDSE), and Department of Aeronautical and Aviation Engineering, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University

Date: May 3, 2026 (Sunday) Venue: M1603 and M1607, HKPolyU Format: In-person with online streaming option

About the Meeting

This one-day satellite meeting is organized in conjunction with the Asia-Pacific Regional IAU Meeting (APRIM2026) to be held in Hong Kong. The meeting focuses on the intersection of precision engineering, space robotics, autonomous systems, and astronomical research, with particular emphasis on lunar, planetary, and small body exploration missions.

The meeting will leverage The Hong Kong Polytechnic University’s strengths in precision engineering, space instrumentation, autonomous navigation, robotics, and deep space exploration to create a platform for knowledge exchange among astronomers, engineers, and space scientists from the Asia-Pacific region and beyond.

Meeting Objectives

  • Showcase Hong Kong’s capabilities and achievements in space robotics, autonomous systems, instrumentation development, and deep space exploration.
  • Foster collaboration between engineers, roboticists, astronomers, and planetary scientists.
  • Explore synergies between precision engineering, space robotics, AI, and astronomical research.
  • Highlight current and future robotic and autonomous missions for lunar, planetary, and small body exploration.
  • Provide networking opportunities for researchers and practitioners in the field.
  • Discuss challenges and solutions for autonomous navigation and operations in deep space.

Themes and Topics

Space Robotics and Mechatronics

  • Robotic systems for planetary surface exploration
  • Sample collection mechanisms and robotics
  • Actuator and sensor technologies for extreme environments
  • Mechatronic systems for space applications
  • Swarm robotics for distributed space exploration

Autonomous Systems for Space Exploration

  • AI and machine learning for spacecraft operations
  • Autonomous navigation and positioning
  • Computer vision for planetary rovers
  • Decision-making under uncertainty in space environments
  • SLAM techniques for unknown planetary environments

Precision Engineering for Space Instrumentation

  • Ultra-precision manufacturing techniques for space instruments
  • Optical systems for space telescopes and observatories
  • Miniaturized systems for small satellites and CubeSats
  • Precision mechanisms for deployment and articulation

Lunar and Planetary Exploration Technologies

  • Surface sampling and in-situ analysis technologies
  • Rover and lander systems for extreme environments
  • Hong Kong and China’s contributions to lunar missions
  • Autonomous drilling and subsurface exploration

Small Body Exploration Challenges

  • Asteroid and comet sampling technologies
  • Remote sensing of small bodies
  • Mission planning and navigation for small body rendezvous
  • Robotic anchoring and mobility in low-gravity environments
